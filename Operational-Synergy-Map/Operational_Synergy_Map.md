# Operational Synergy Map - Overview

## Definition

The **Operational Synergy Map (OSM)** coordinates resources, teams, processes, tools, and operational interfaces in order to ensure smooth, efficient, and reliable system and platform operations.

It serves as the operational coordination layer that translates strategic and architectural decisions into practical execution structures and day-to-day operating effectiveness.

---

## Objective

The objective of the Operational Synergy Map is to ensure smooth and efficient operations across teams, resources, and execution processes.

---

## Purpose

The Operational Synergy Map provides the methods for coordinating the operational side of implementation and delivery.

Its purpose is to ensure that:

- resources are planned realistically
- teams are aligned and effectively coordinated
- operational processes run efficiently
- tools and technologies are used coherently
- responsibilities and dependencies are transparent
- implementation can be sustained in daily practice

The Operational Synergy Map is therefore a living operational coordination model that helps maintain execution quality, delivery continuity, and agile organizational effectiveness.

---

## Core Role within the Agile Innovation Framework

The Operational Synergy Map complements the other framework elements by focusing on operational execution and coordination.

### Relationship to the Innovation Blueprint

The Innovation Blueprint defines what needs to be built and implemented. The **Operational Synergy Map** helps organize **how operational resources**, teams, and processes **support** that **realization** in practice.

### Relationship to the Visionary Execution Roadmap

The Roadmap defines the **time-based sequence of implementation**. The Operational Synergy Map aligns operational capacity, team coordination, and process readiness with that sequence.

### Relationship to the Technological Architecture Manifesto

The Technological Architecture Manifesto defines validated architecture and technology choices. The Operational Synergy Map **documents how** those **validated technologies are integrated into operational workflows** and where interim arrangements or transition measures are required.

### Relationship to the Compliance & Security Doctrine

The Compliance & Security Doctrine defines security and compliance constraints. The Operational Synergy Map **ensures that operational processes, responsibilities, and tools are aligned with those constraints in practice**.

## Core Elements of Operational Synergy Map

## 1. Resource Planning

### Description

This section **provides detailed planning** of the operational resources **required for implementation, coordination, delivery, and ongoing operation**.

### Content

- allocation of people and roles
- capacity planning
- budget-related operational constraints
- infrastructure and environment needs
- dependency-aware resource assignment
- planning assumptions for operational continuity

The goal is to **ensure that resources are assigned where they create the highest operational value** and that **bottlenecks are recognized early**.

---

## 2. Team Coordination

### Description

This section defines the strategies and structures required to coordinate different teams effectively.

### Content

- role clarity and ownership
- cross-team communication structures
- coordination mechanisms between technical and non-technical functions
- dependency management between teams
- escalation paths and decision channels
- alignment rituals and governance interfaces

The purpose is to enable teams to work as a coherent operational system rather than as isolated units.

---

## 3. Process Optimization

### Description

This section defines methods for continuously improving operational workflows and execution quality.

### Content

- identification of bottlenecks and inefficiencies
- improvement of handovers and coordination points
- reduction of unnecessary operational friction
- standardization of recurring workflows where useful
- iterative refinement of operating procedures
- learning loops based on feedback and operational evidence

The objective is to keep operations adaptive, efficient, and aligned with changing implementation realities.

---

## 4. Tools and Technologies

### Description

This section lists and contextualizes the tools and technologies used to support operational execution.

### Content

- collaboration tools
- planning and tracking tools
- integration and delivery tooling
- monitoring and observability tools
- governance and documentation tooling
- validation, reporting, and audit-support tooling

The purpose is not merely to inventory tools, but to clarify how they support operational coordination, delivery, and continuous improvement.

---

## 5. Documentation

### Description

The Operational Synergy Map should be documented in structured and operationally useful formats.

### Typical Artifacts

- organizational chart
- team coordination maps
- role and responsibility models
- process flow diagrams
- resource plans
- dependency maps
- operating procedures
- workflow visualizations

Documentation creates **transparency** and **enables repeatable, reviewable operations** as well as **continous optimization**.

---

## Effort Estimation and Operational Planning

The Operational Synergy Map is also the appropriate place to document operational effort estimates and resource implications related to implementation.

### Purpose

It records:

- which resources are needed for which tasks
- how these resources are coordinated
- where effort concentrations exist
- which functions or teams are capacity-critical
- how effort assumptions affect operational planning

This makes operational readiness visible and supports realistic execution decisions.

## Typical Focus Areas

### Resource Allocation

The Operational Synergy Map should clarify how resources are distributed across:

- implementation activities
- support functions
- governance activities
- operational maintenance
- improvement cycles

### Coordination of Responsibilities

The Operational Synergy Map should make clear:

- who is responsible for what
- where ownership starts and ends
- how coordination across interfaces works
- which decisions require joint alignment

### Operational Readiness

The Operational Synergy Map should indicate whether teams, tools, processes, and environments are sufficiently prepared to support reliable execution and ongoing operation.

## Process Perspective

The Operational Synergy Map should support both structured planning and operational adaptability.

### Key Questions

- Which teams need to collaborate for smooth execution?
- Which resources are required for which activities?
- Where are the key operational dependencies?
- Which processes need optimization?
- Which tools support coordination and delivery?
- Where are the bottlenecks, risks, or inefficiencies in operations?
- How can effort and resource planning be kept transparent and realistic?

## Iterative Character

The Operational Synergy Map should be maintained as a living coordination model.

It should be reviewed and adjusted regularly based on:

- changing priorities
- resource shifts
- new dependencies
- process learnings
- delivery bottlenecks
- operational feedback

This ensures that operational coordination remains relevant, effective, and aligned with the evolving implementation context.

The Operational Synergy Map should be guided by principles such as:

- efficient use of resources

- adaptability in execution
- dependency awareness
- process coherence

## Summary

The **Operational Synergy Map (OSM)** coordinates resources, teams, processes, and supporting tools in order to ensure smooth and efficient operations.

It provides the framework for:

- resource planning
- team coordination
- process optimization
- operational tool alignment
- effort estimation
- structured documentation

Its role is to ensure that implementation and operations are not only strategically planned and technically defined, but also operationally coordinated and sustainably executable in practice.
