# Innovation Matrix - Overview

## Definition

The **Innovation Matrix** prioritizes development initiatives based on their **strategic value**, **implementation effort**, and **available resources** in order to deliver the most valuable capabilities first.

---

## Objective

The objective of the Innovation Matrix is to prioritize development initiatives based on their strategic importance and the resources available for implementation.

---

## Purpose

The Innovation Matrix provides a continuously current overview of:

- implementation effort
- priorities
- strategic relevance
- resource implications

Its purpose is to support structured decision-making by evaluating development initiatives according to **value**, **complexity**, and **resource demand**. This ensures that the most important and most valuable capabilities are implemented first.

---

## Distinction from the Innovation Blueprint

The **Innovation Blueprint** describes the functional and technical requirements of a platform, product, or system.

The **Innovation Matrix** goes one step further by prioritizing those requirements and Features according to:

- strategic benefit
- implementation effort
- required resources
- relative urgency
- expected impact

In other words, the Innovation Blueprint explains **what needs to be built**, while the Innovation Matrix answers:

> **What should be built first, and why?**

It therefore serves as a decision-making model for determining which Features or initiatives should be implemented, in which order, and on what basis.

---

## Strategic Relevance

The Innovation Matrix is an essential instrument for ensuring that resources are used efficiently and that development capacity is focused on the most valuable and strategically important initiatives.

It enables dynamic adjustment when:

- requirements change
- priorities shift
- resources become constrained or newly available
- market or technical conditions evolve

---

## Core Content

### 1. Effort Estimation

The Innovation Matrix includes a detailed analysis of the effort required to implement Features, Use Cases, or other development initiatives.

This may include:

- development effort
- integration effort
- operational effort
- coordination overhead
- maintenance implications

### 2. Strategic Priorities

Each initiative is evaluated according to its strategic value.

This assessment helps determine how strongly a Feature, Use Case, or requirement contributes to:

- business goals
- competitive differentiation
- platform capability
- long-term vision
- operational effectiveness

### 3. Matrix Visualization

The Innovation Matrix visualizes priorities in a structured matrix format.

Typical dimensions may include:

- value vs. effort
- strategic relevance vs. complexity
- benefit vs. resource demand
- impact vs. feasibility

This visualization supports clear prioritization and transparent communication.

### 4. Resource Allocation

The Innovation Matrix supports planning and assigning resources based on strategic priority and implementation feasibility.

This includes:

- team allocation
- sequencing of work
- budget awareness
- dependency-sensitive planning
- capacity alignment

### 5. Documentation

The Innovation Matrix should be documented in a structured and reviewable form.

Typical artifacts include:

- innovation matrix tables
- prioritization overviews
- effort assessments
- resource plans
- decision rationale

---

## Evaluation of Use Cases

The Innovation Matrix also supports the evaluation and prioritization of **Use Cases**.

### Purpose of Use Case Evaluation

Use Cases are prioritized based on:

- their contribution to the relevant Epics
- their strategic significance
- their implementation cost
- their expected value and benefit

This ensures that operational scenarios are not evaluated in isolation, but in direct relation to strategic goals and execution priorities.

### Evaluation Dimensions

Typical dimensions for Use Case evaluation include:

- alignment with Epic objectives
- user relevance
- operational value
- technical feasibility
- implementation effort
- expected benefit

---

## Metrics and KPIs

The Innovation Matrix should be supported by measurable indicators in order to make prioritization transparent and evidence-based.

### Example Metrics and KPIs

#### Progress of Use Cases
Measures implementation progress as an indicator of how effectively Epic-level goals are being realized.

#### User Acquisition
Tracks the ability of implemented capabilities to attract new users, customers, or stakeholders.

#### Interaction Rates
Measures how actively users interact with the platform, product, or system.

#### Delivery Efficiency
Assesses how efficiently prioritized initiatives are implemented relative to effort and capacity.

#### Strategic Alignment Score
Evaluates how closely an initiative supports strategic goals and long-term direction.

#### Value Realization Indicator
Measures whether expected strategic or operational value is actually being achieved.

---

## Decision Logic

The Innovation Matrix supports prioritization decisions by combining strategic and operational perspectives.

### Key Questions

- Which initiative creates the highest strategic value?
- Which initiative delivers the strongest benefit relative to effort?
- Which initiative is feasible with currently available resources?
- Which initiative should be implemented first to unlock further value?
- Which initiative has dependencies that affect sequencing?

---

## Relationship to Other Framework Elements

The Innovation Matrix is closely connected to other planning and execution elements.

### Relationship to Epics
Epics define the high-level strategic direction. The Innovation Matrix helps determine which initiatives contribute most strongly to those goals and should therefore be prioritized.

### Relationship to the Innovation Blueprint
The Innovation Blueprint defines the operational content of implementation. The Innovation Matrix prioritizes that content.

### Relationship to Roadmaps
Roadmaps translate prioritization into time-based sequencing. The Innovation Matrix provides the basis for those sequencing decisions.

### Relationship to Resource Planning
Resource planning operationalizes the decisions made through the Innovation Matrix by aligning people, budgets, and capacities with strategic priorities.

---

## Practical Function

In practice, the Innovation Matrix serves as a living prioritization instrument.

It should be reviewed and updated regularly in response to:

- newly identified requirements
- changing strategic goals
- implementation learnings
- capacity changes
- market feedback
- technology shifts

This ensures that prioritization remains relevant, adaptive, and strategically grounded.

---

## Summary

The **Innovation Matrix** is a prioritization framework for development initiatives. It evaluates and ranks Features, Use Cases, and related implementation elements based on:

- strategic value
- required effort
- available resources
- expected benefit
- implementation feasibility

Its role is to ensure that development capacity is focused on the most valuable and strategically relevant initiatives first.

While the Innovation Blueprint defines **what must be built**, the Innovation Matrix determines **what should be built first and why**.
