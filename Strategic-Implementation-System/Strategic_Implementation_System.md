# Strategic Implementation System (SIS)

## Overview

The **Strategic Implementation System (SIS)** represents the current state and the systematic planning layer for execution. It operationalizes strategic goals, prioritizes them, and monitors progress along the **Visionary Execution Roadmap**.

The SIS differs from traditional project plans through its focus on the dynamic integration of strategic objectives, technical evolution, and operational execution.

---

## Agile Elements

### Epics

Epics serve as the primary agile element within the SIS. They define high-level strategic objectives, long-term milestones, and cross-cutting themes that guide implementation.

---

## Purpose of the SIS

The SIS provides a structured system to:

- translate strategy into execution
- align initiatives with long-term goals
- prioritize strategic work
- coordinate resources and responsibilities
- monitor progress and dependencies
- optimize implementation continuously

---

## Core Content of the SIS

### 1. Introduction

The SIS is not a static project plan. It is a dynamic system that connects strategic direction, technical architecture, and operational delivery. Its purpose is to ensure that planning remains coherent, adaptive, and execution-oriented.

### 2. Timelines and Milestones

The SIS includes detailed milestone planning and time horizons to enable phased, prioritized, and transparent implementation.

### 3. Resource Management

The SIS provides an overview of resource allocation, including:

- team structure
- budget planning
- tools and platforms in use

Its purpose is to ensure efficient and goal-oriented use of resources.

### 4. Risk Assessment

The SIS identifies potential risks and defines strategies to minimize impact on:

- timelines
- costs
- quality
- operational continuity

### 5. Implementation Strategies

The SIS includes methods and approaches that support effective execution, such as:

- agile processes
- iterative prototyping
- structured validation
- incremental delivery

### 6. Documentation

The SIS visualizes progress, milestones, and dependencies in transparent and reviewable formats, including:

- tables
- diagrams
- flowcharts
- roadmaps

## Epics

### Definition

Epics are large, high-level requirements or initiatives that describe a major capability or a significant system objective. They encompass multiple User Stories and/or Features and act as a structural framework that can be broken down into smaller, executable units.

Epics describe the **what at a high level**.

They are narrative by nature. They define major objectives and scope without going into detailed implementation. Their role is to communicate the broader purpose and vision that will later be realized through more specific requirements, interactions, and delivery artifacts.

### Strategic Role of Epics

Epics are:

- long-term milestones
- overarching strategic themes
- cross-functional implementation anchors
- the top-level framework for strategic implementation

In **every organisation**, Epics represent the key strategic themes and primary objectives to be achieved. They are less detailed than Use Cases, Features, and User Stories and provide a clear view of the strategic direction of development.

### Example

An Epic related to [DSL-Core](https://github.com/rock-the-prototype/dsl-core)is:

> The development of a fully auditable software development process that includes AI-supported quality control, requirements management, and system security.

---

## Hierarchy and Sequence

The SIS follows a clear structural sequence:

1. **Epics** are defined first and establish the strategic direction.
2. **Use Cases** are then added through the **Innovation Blueprint** to clarify how users will interact with the platform and systems.
3. **User Stories** are derived to identify and document specific user needs.
4. **Features** are developed to fulfill the User Stories and enable the workflows described in the Use Cases.

This structure ensures that your epics cover both high-level strategic requirements and detailed technical functionality, while consistently keeping end-user value in focus.

## Prioritization of Epics in the SIF

### Timing of Prioritization

Epics are prioritized:

- after they are defined
- before they are detailed into Use Cases, Features, and User Stories
- before resource planning and allocation in the **Visionary Execution Roadmap**

---

## Prioritization Criteria for Epics

### 1. Strategic Significance

#### Alignment with Business Goals
How strongly does the Epic support the strategic goals of **your organisation**?

#### Market Potential
What effect does the Epic have on market positioning and competitive advantage?

### 2. Resource Demand

#### Cost
What level of investment is required to implement the Epic?

#### Resource Availability
Are the required resources available, including people, technology, and infrastructure?

### 3. Technical Feasibility

#### Technology Maturity
How mature is the technology required for implementation?

#### Integration Fit
How well can the Epic be integrated into existing systems and architectures?

### 4. Timeline and Urgency

#### Time to Market
How quickly must the Epic be implemented to respond to market change or secure strategic advantage?

#### Dependencies
Are there dependencies between Epics that affect priority?

### 5. Risk Management

#### Implementation Risk
What is the likelihood that implementation may fail or underdeliver?

#### Impact of Non-Delivery
What are the consequences if the Epic is delayed or not delivered?

### 6. Customer Value and Market Demand

#### Customer Demand
How strong is demand for the capability or solution enabled by the Epic?

#### Competitive Differentiation
To what extent does the Epic strengthen differentiation from competitors?

---

## KPIs for Epic Prioritization

The following KPIs support structured Epic prioritization:

- **Return on Investment (ROI):** expected economic benefit relative to implementation cost
- **Time to Market (TTM):** time required to implement and launch the Epic
- **Resource Allocation Efficiency:** efficiency of resource use relative to Epic priority
- **Customer Impact Score:** expected effect on customer satisfaction and market position
- **Risk Mitigation Index:** degree to which the Epic reduces or avoids relevant risks
- **Alignment Score:** level of fit with the strategic goals and vision of your organisation

---

## Prioritization Process

### 1. Evaluation

Each Epic is assessed against the defined criteria and KPIs.

### 2. Scoring

A scoring method, such as a point-based scale, is applied to evaluate each Epic objectively.

### 3. Priority List

A ranked Epic list is created based on the scoring results.

### 4. Review and Adjustment

The priority list is reviewed regularly and adjusted when market conditions, technical realities, or strategic goals change.

This structured prioritization approach ensures that **Rock the Project** focuses its resources on the most important and valuable initiatives in order to maximize impact and long-term success.

## Summary

The **Strategic Implementation System (SIS)** is the operational bridge between strategy and execution. It provides the structure needed to align Epics, Use Cases, User Stories, Features, resources, timelines, and risks within a single coherent implementation system.

Its purpose is not only to plan work, but to ensure that execution remains strategically aligned, adaptive, transparent, and measurable over time.
